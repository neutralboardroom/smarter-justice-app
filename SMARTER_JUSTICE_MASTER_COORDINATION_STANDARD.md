# Smarter Justice Master Coordination Standard

Version 1.3.3

The Smarter Justice Master Coordination chat and track govern ecosystem strategy, shared systems, professional-marketplace policy, portfolio priorities, and the private builder dashboard. Detailed implementation remains in a dedicated development chat for each portal.

## Master Coordination scope

- overall ecosystem strategy and portal priorities;
- shared architecture, design-system direction, interoperability, and reusable modules;
- customer, owner, staff, paralegal, reviewer, professional, referral, and firm dashboard standards;
- forms, official sources, jurisdictions, readiness, security, privacy, AI, compliance, pricing, account, and cross-portal standards;
- Master Rules and Suggestions Pack governance, protected retrieval, checksum, and change history;
- private Control Center, real-package inventory, roadmap, progress, risks, blockers, test evidence, and launch readiness;
- central professional and firm accounts, source profiles, claims, credentials, memberships, consultations, consent, sponsorship, reviews, and revenue;
- shared registries, analytics, integrations, deployment tracking, and later safe automation;
- decisions about which portal to build or improve next.

## Portal implementation boundary

Each portal’s dedicated chat should contain its latest complete continuation prompt, latest ZIP or package set, specialty decisions, forms and official sources, workflows, pricing, professional roles, tests, deployment history, limitations, and next build. The Master Coordination track should receive a concise Control Center update after every release.

## Package-role tracking

The Control Center must distinguish complete deployable packages, archival/full bases, lean continuations, overlays, historical references, and do-not-clean-deploy packages. Immigration Oasis is the governing example: its effective v1.10.227 build requires the v1.10.162 full base plus the v1.10.227 lean overlay.

## Shared defaults and specialty authority

Shared standards are strong defaults, not inflexible rules. A portal builder may deliberately and narrowly adapt a shared pattern when specialty, jurisdiction, professional duty, evidence, urgency, safety, usability, compliance, or technical requirements justify it. Record default, reason, scope, risks, mitigation, and benefit. Do not create differences merely for variety.

## Governance inheritance

Every generated Master Coordination prompt and portal continuation prompt must embed the current Master Rules Pack, identify its version and checksum, and preserve the newest portal-specific source of truth. The newest approved portal prompt and newest uploaded ZIP govern unless deliberately superseded.

## Professional network coordination

Professionals and firms should manage approved marketplace identities centrally through Smarter Justice across multiple practice areas and micro-portals. Maintain common status definitions, source evidence, owner-approved claims, membership eligibility, consent, sponsorship separation, revenue tracking, and professional independence. Specialty portals may add specialty fields and services without creating conflicting identities.

## Directory, source, and onboarding coordination

The Master Coordination track must preserve evidence that professional and firm directories use useful default result sizes, working pagination, clear and shareable non-sensitive filters, honest source availability, source-grounded profile metadata, connected claim and approval flows, understandable onboarding progress, transparent firm pricing estimates, and accurate acquisition attribution. A successful one-record test is not sufficient.

## Founding-member and firm-sales coordination

Coordinate NYC in-person outreach, campaign and QR attribution, $10–$20 founding-price tests, Stripe-confirmed subscriptions, firm per-seat billing, volume discounts, central firm administration, conversion metrics, and future tiers. Payment never replaces credentials, approval, availability, or compliance and never affects substantive AI analysis or neutral eligibility.

## Release coordination requirement

Every portal release should return an updated manifest, release notes, full continuation prompt, clean artifact, test results, rules-pack version/checksum, Control Center update summary, package-role record, specialty adaptations, and unresolved blockers.

## Professional marketplace release coordination

The Master Coordination track must monitor useful default result sizes, pagination, individual and firm profile parity, claim-control approval, accurate campaign attribution, checkout truthfulness, and phone/tablet acquisition evidence. These requirements belong in future portal prompts whenever a portal consumes the shared professional marketplace.
