FAQ

Plain answers before you start.

Is Smarter Justice a law firm?

No. Smarter Justice is a private support service, not a law firm and not the government.

Are government forms free?

Official government forms are often free from the government. Smarter Justice charges only for support, organization, review, and preparation services where available.

Can I upload a notice or letter?

Yes. Smarter Justice supports uploads for PDFs, screenshots, photos, emails, notices, government letters, user documents, tax notices, benefits notices, accident/insurance letters, demand letters, lawsuits, and urgent issues.

What is a Human Review Specialist?

A reviewer who can organize the file, summarize documents, ask follow-up questions, and prepare starter files. This is separate from attorney or professional review.

What tax help is included?

The Taxes area includes tax preparation and tax resolution. Tax resolution includes IRS/state notices, audits, liens, levies, installment agreements, penalty relief, offers in compromise, and tax debt organization.

Do I need to choose my state?

For state-law or local-law matters, yes. Divorce, housing, probate, real estate, workers’ compensation, personal injury, malpractice, name change, expungement, employment, and similar issues require state/local context.

What is a Community Partner?

A Community Partner is a person, business, organization, event, or community location that helps someone start Smarter Justice and receive a private continuation link. Community Partners do not give legal advice, promise outcomes, or complete the application for the user.

Can Smarter Justice guarantee results?

No. Smarter Justice does not guarantee approval, timing, agency refunds, benefits, tax outcomes, legal outcomes, settlements, or case results.

Will every form be automated on day one?

No. Smarter Justice supports broad starting questions, document organization, and review recommendations. Completed-form preparation is offered only where the path is verified and appropriate. Other matters receive worksheets, organized starter files, summaries, and review recommendations.
