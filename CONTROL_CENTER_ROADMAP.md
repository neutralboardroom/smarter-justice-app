# Smarter Justice Control Center Roadmap

## Current release: v1.6.1

The private owner Control Center currently manages:

- 23 current or planned portal records;
- real ZIP/version/package-role tracking for six uploaded platforms;
- portal progress, releases, tests, blockers, risks, readiness, continuation prompts, and next builds;
- Master Rules and Suggestions Pack 1.4.3 and protected API;
- public-source professional and firm records;
- central professional-account summaries and pending profile claims;
- professional and firm membership plans;
- firm seat quotes and volume discounts;
- professional credentials, services, jurisdictions, portal mappings, and eligibility;
- NYC outreach campaigns, buildings, firms, prospects, follow-up, seats, and projected recurring revenue;
- revenue-program status and compliance gates;
- profile claim, correction, privacy, removal, and credential-update requests;
- official New York attorney source preview and controlled import.

Customer matter files are not displayed in the owner portfolio/marketplace workspace.

## Controlled-launch security and operating controls added in v1.6.0

- Fail-closed production persistence and explicit storage-readiness evidence
- Owner account sessions, authenticator MFA, recovery codes, and revocation
- Professional password recovery, MFA, recovery codes, and revocation
- Paused-by-default founding-member cohort controls and capacity limits
- Manual-first credential verification, complaints, compliance holds, suspension, and restoration
- Cross-Portal Capability Registry and Success Pattern Library

Next evidence gates are production configuration, Stripe and SMTP lifecycle testing, backup/restore, real-device and QR enrollment, legal/ethics procedures, and first-cohort approval.

## Non-negotiable sequencing

Do not activate public booking, public reviews, automated professional recommendations, user-document sharing, or broad professional opportunity routing before authentication, MFA, permissions, auditing, consent, conflicts, correction, moderation, credential, legal, ethics, privacy, security, and incident controls are mature.

## Release-quality governance added in v1.5.1

- Track public-language readiness, mobile-device readiness, accessibility readiness, signup-and-conversion readiness, and prompt/build-handoff readiness for every portal.
- Keep technical readiness, staff operations, owner evidence, and deployment details on protected surfaces rather than customer navigation or public sitemaps.
- Require every replacement continuation prompt to report these readiness fields and preserve the current Master Rules Pack version and checksum.
- Record the available-now versus planned status of every professional marketplace feature.

## Official-source, profile-discovery, onboarding, and pricing controls added in v1.5.3

The Control Center and generated handoffs now require evidence for:

- official-source pagination with offsets and preserved filters;
- useful result counts, progress, clear controls, and honest unavailable-source states;
- shareable public directory filters only when they contain no private matter data;
- source-grounded titles, descriptions, canonical URLs, and structured profile metadata;
- connected professional onboarding progress across account, claim, approval, public information, membership, credentials, services, agreements, and opportunity eligibility;
- phone-friendly autofill and input modes for enrollment;
- transparent firm seat and discount estimates that remain subordinate to final checkout terms;
- clear free-versus-paid public pricing and separate government, filing, third-party, human-review, and professional charges.

## Directory completeness and firm-profile parity added in v1.5.2

The Control Center and future build prompts now require evidence for useful default directory page sizes, accurate totals, pagination or show-more controls, multi-record professional and firm rendering, firm detail pages, firm claim approval, post-approval editing, accurate campaign attribution, vendor-neutral public checkout wording, and narrow-phone acquisition behavior.
