# Smarter Justice Public Professional Profile Source Standard

Version 1.1.1

## Governing rule

Initial attorney, firm, CPA, enrolled-agent, accountant, tax-attorney, and other approved professional records may be created from lawfully available public information when the source use is appropriate. The preferred model combines curated source-tracked profiles with live approved official-data lookup instead of freezing a stale nationwide directory into each release ZIP.

## Required source record

Each imported fact must be traceable to a source record containing source name, publisher, URL or dataset identifier, authority level, retrieval date, supported facts, external identifier where available, review status, use/terms notes, and last-verification information.

## New York attorney source

The approved New York connector uses the New York State Unified Court System attorney-registration dataset through its source-specific New York Open Data endpoint. The connector rejects arbitrary source URLs, maps only supported public fields, records retrieval evidence, and never infers practice specialty from registration data alone. The individual court search may be used for verification and corrections.

## Seeded and live-result status

A seeded or live official result remains unclaimed unless control is verified. It may be searchable, but it must not receive inferred membership, participation, availability, service price, consultation eligibility, endorsement, quality, or trust. The public page must identify the source and explain the limitation.

## Claims and edits

A claim request does not grant editing access. Identity and authority must be reviewed, and owner approval must explicitly connect the profile to the central professional account. Professional-submitted edits remain distinguishable from official and secondary-source facts. Credential verification and marketplace eligibility remain independent.

## Conservative mapping

Do not infer practice area, quality, language, office coverage, disciplinary conclusions, or professional authority from unsupported data. Firm websites and professional pages may support self-described practice areas, biographies, locations, and services when source-labeled and reviewed.

## Rights and operations

Support claim, correction, credential update, affiliation update, privacy, and legally appropriate removal requests. Preserve historical source and decision records. Do not scrape or republish data in violation of law, terms, privacy rules, or professional regulation. Reverify high-impact facts and display last-checked dates where practical.

## Public experience rule

Customer and professional-facing pages must use plain language, clearly separate available features from planned capabilities, and be reviewed on phone, tablet, and desktop before release. Internal eligibility logic, configuration, technical evidence, and staff operations remain on protected surfaces.

## Firm-source parity

Firm profiles require the same source traceability, retrieval dates, supported-fact limits, unclaimed labeling, correction path, and claim-control review as individual profiles. A firm claim must verify authority to act for the organization before editing access is granted.
