# Smarter Justice Professional Marketplace Standard

Version 1.3.2

## Purpose

Combine AI-guided matter organization, public professional discovery, central profile management, fixed-fee memberships, consultation services, limited review, and professional engagement without becoming a generic directory or allowing payment to distort substantive guidance.

## Central identity and account rule

A professional or firm should manage its Smarter Justice identity from one central Smarter Justice account. The account may control multiple approved profiles, firm seats, practice areas, locations, services, and micro-portal participation. Specialty portals consume the approved central record and may add specialty-specific settings, but should not create conflicting identities or separate billing accounts without a documented reason.

## Status separation

Listing, public visibility, claim, account control, credential verification, paid membership, participation, service activation, sponsorship, reviews, and consultation eligibility are separate facts. No one status implies another.

## Public-source and claim rules

A public-source profile may be searchable while remaining unclaimed. Listing does not imply endorsement, membership, participation, availability, or quality. A claim request never grants edit rights. Control begins only after identity and authority review and explicit owner approval. Credentials, membership, service activation, and consultation eligibility remain separate after control is approved.

## Paid membership rule

A free or unclaimed listing may exist without marketplace participation. A professional must generally be an active qualifying paid individual member or a covered professional seat of an approved paid firm member to receive free or paid consultation bookings, document-review assignments, or structured opportunities. Payment alone is insufficient.

## Independent eligibility gates

Eligibility also requires current appropriate credentials, identity verification, applicable jurisdiction, relevant active service and portal, availability, accepted marketplace and conflict policies, owner approval, and no suspension or blocking compliance issue.

## Directory completeness, discoverability, and onboarding

- Official-source and platform directories must use useful bounded defaults, working offset or cursor pagination, preserved search filters, accurate shown-result progress, and clear reset controls.
- Shareable public directory URLs may contain only non-sensitive search filters.
- Dynamic profile titles, descriptions, canonical URLs, and structured data must be supported by approved profile facts and must not invent specialization, quality, participation, availability, or endorsement.
- Professional dashboards should show a plain-language setup checklist separating account creation, profile or firm control, public information, membership, credentials, agreements, services, availability, and opportunity eligibility.
- Mobile enrollment should use useful autofill attributes, appropriate keyboards, clear recurring-price disclosures, and transparent firm-seat estimates.

## Neutral discovery and sponsorship

AI and rules may identify the type of help, professional category, jurisdiction, urgency, language, and service needed. Compensation and sponsorship may not control that analysis. Users choose among neutrally eligible providers. Sponsored placement must be clearly labeled and separate from credential, rating, risk, and eligibility systems.

## Membership billing

Individual and firm memberships use fixed subscription pricing. Stripe activation must occur only after a verified Checkout session or signed webhook confirms payment. Missing credentials or incomplete payment must leave membership inactive. Firm plans may use per-seat billing, centralized administration, and transparent volume discounts; each covered professional still passes individual eligibility gates.

## Data sharing and engagement

Do not share user identity, matter summaries, deadlines, or documents with a professional without explicit recorded purpose-limited consent and conflict/access controls. Booking does not itself create an attorney-client, accountant-client, tax-client, or other professional relationship. Engagement, conflicts, scope, terms, and acceptance remain separate.

## Fees and independence

Use fixed subscriptions, fixed platform charges, and clearly labeled advertising first. Do not share legal or professional fees, settlements, recoveries, refunds, or outcomes unless the exact arrangement receives jurisdiction-specific legal and ethics approval. Preserve independent professional judgment.

## New York City founding-member pilot

The controlled NYC pilot may use $15 monthly or $150 annual individual pricing within the approved $10–$20 test range, with firm per-seat pricing and volume discounts. The offer is introductory, not a permanent promise, and may not promise users, appointments, exclusivity, results, ranking, or future pricing.

The pilot supports in-person building and firm outreach, campaign and QR attribution, mobile signup, central profile claim/editing, firm roster management, private follow-up, and owner sales-pipeline tracking. Outreach must respect building access, solicitation, privacy, advertising, and professional-conduct requirements.

## Firm discounts

Suggested founding tiers are 10% for 2–4 seats, 15% for 5–9, 20% for 10–24, and 25% for 25 or more. Firm discounts and premium profiles never affect AI analysis, credential verification, ratings, warnings, or neutral eligibility.

## Activation stages

1. Private architecture and source governance.
2. Controlled NYC founding-professional pilot.
3. Public directory and paid membership activation.
4. Consultation scheduling and curated service pilot.
5. AI-guided professional marketplace and consent-controlled handoff.
6. Full professional operating system, integrations, analytics, and enterprise tools.

## Public experience rule

Customer and professional-facing pages must use plain language, clearly separate available features from planned capabilities, and be reviewed on phone, tablet, and desktop before release. Internal eligibility logic, configuration, technical evidence, and staff operations remain on protected surfaces.

## Directory completeness and firm parity

- Use a useful documented default page size rather than an accidental one-record default.
- Return accurate totals and offsets and provide pagination or a clear show-more action.
- Test multi-record individual and firm results, not only a single profile detail.
- Give firm profiles source, claim, correction, approval, account-control, editing, and public-status protections equivalent to individual profiles.
- Do not assign a location-specific campaign to general website signups unless the user entered through that campaign.
- Use vendor-neutral public checkout language unless the payment processor name materially helps the user make an informed decision.
