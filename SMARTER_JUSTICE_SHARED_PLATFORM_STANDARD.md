# Smarter Justice Shared Platform Standard

Version 1.3.3

## Operating model

Use one Smarter Justice Master Coordination track for ecosystem strategy and a dedicated development chat for each portal’s detailed implementation. Keep each portal separately branded, deployable, versioned, testable, reversible, and capable of specialty-specific adaptation.

## Strong defaults, not inflexible rules

Shared standards are strong defaults, not inflexible rules. A portal may adapt design, terminology, dashboards, forms, pricing, roles, workflows, evidence, data models, security controls, or technical patterns when specialty, jurisdiction, users, urgency, professional duties, compliance, safety, usability, or integrations justify a better approach.

Every adaptation must identify the default, reason, scope, risks, mitigations, and expected benefit. It must be preserved in future prompts until superseded. It may not weaken privacy, security, truthful public language, professional independence, source verification, consent, or legal and regulatory safeguards.

## Shared customer and operational standards

- Plain customer-facing language; avoid internal queue, engine, handoff, release-gate, risk-flag, or developer terminology.
- Honest portal, service, payment, AI, form, review, professional, and launch statuses.
- State, county, city, court, agency, form-year, edition, and jurisdiction awareness.
- Clear separation of AI assistance, Human Review Specialist work, paralegal preparation, and licensed professional services.
- No-guess flows that preserve uncertainty and answer-later states.
- Preparation, draft, payment, review, download, signature, filing, submission, receipt, and acceptance remain separate states.
- Green/Yellow/Red or specialty-adapted readiness lanes where useful.
- Secure uploads, private continuation access, data minimization, auditability, durable production storage, and clean release artifacts.
- Source-tracked official forms, instructions, fees, credentials, and public profile facts.
- Evidence-backed release ledgers and page-by-page QA before official-form completion claims.
- Neutral transactional email, text, and calendar content without sensitive matter details.
- Community Partner terminology and privacy-protective referral reporting.
- Mobile, accessibility, health, version, deployment, backup/restore, and regression-test standards.
- Controlled pilots with capacity, cohort, manual QA, launch evidence, pause, rollback, and owner go/no-go controls.
- Public directories and official-source finders must use useful bounded defaults, working pagination, preserved filters, clear controls, honest result progress, and reliable multi-record tests.
- Non-sensitive public filters should be shareable when useful; private matter, account, payment, or credential data must never be placed in public URLs.
- Public professional and firm pages should use source-grounded titles, descriptions, canonical URLs, and structured metadata without inventing specialties, participation, quality, or availability.
- Professional onboarding should show understandable progress across account, claim, approval, public information, membership, credentials, services, agreements, and opportunity eligibility.
- Public pricing should distinguish genuinely free AI-guided help from optional human review, professional services, government, filing, and third-party charges.
- No changes merely for the sake of change or superficial consistency.

## Master Rules Pack inheritance

Every continuation prompt, replacement prompt, build handoff, and portal manifest must include or reference the current Master Rules and Suggestions Pack, record its version and checksum, and preserve the complete portal-specific source of truth. Approved portals may retrieve it through the protected versioned API and should retain a checksummed local copy.

## Central professional marketplace defaults

- One central Smarter Justice account should manage approved professional profiles, firm seats, practice areas, services, and micro-portal participation.
- Public listing, claim request, account control, verification, membership, participation, sponsorship, reviews, and consultation eligibility are separate states.
- Public-source profiles are source-tracked, conservative, and unclaimed; listing never implies endorsement, participation, availability, membership, or quality.
- A claim request remains read-only until identity/authority review and explicit owner approval.
- Active qualifying paid membership is generally required for integrated opportunities, but credentials, jurisdiction, services, agreements, availability, approval, and compliance remain independent gates.
- Firm billing may cover seats, but each professional is independently verified and eligible.
- Sponsorship and payment never control AI analysis, warnings, verification, ratings, or neutral eligibility.
- Users choose among neutrally eligible professionals.
- Matter data and documents require recorded purpose-limited consent and conflict/access controls.
- Booking does not itself create a professional relationship.

## Shared revenue capabilities

Preserve individual and firm memberships, enhanced profiles, labeled sponsorship, scheduling and dashboards, review workflows, analytics, staff seats, practice-growth tools, compliant fixed platform charges, integrations, and white-label/enterprise services. Each capability needs an honest activation status and compliance gate. A portal may omit or adapt a feature when its specialty requires it.

## Public-funded-by-professional freemium default

Where safe and sustainable, core AI-guided public help should be free or very low cost. Government fees, court fees, taxes, human review, professional services, filing assistance, and third-party charges remain separate. Use fair-use and model-routing controls rather than bait-and-switch paywalls. Professional payments never influence substantive guidance.

## NYC professional-network go-to-market default

Relevant portal prompts should inherit the founding-member strategy: low-friction fixed-price membership, Stripe-confirmed recurring payment, per-professional firm seats, transparent volume discounts, mobile and QR enrollment, public-source profile discovery, central profile management, campaign attribution, and owner sales-pipeline tracking. Specialty adaptations are allowed when documented.

## Release coordination

Every release should produce a clean tested package, release notes, updated manifest, complete continuation prompt, Control Center update, documented adaptations, package-role/deployment rules, and an honest list of credentials, integrations, legal review, and launch blockers.

## Directory completeness and firm-profile parity

Public professional and firm searches must return a useful documented default page size, accurate totals, and pagination or show-more controls. Individual and firm records require source, claim, correction, approval, account-control, edit-authorization, and public-status safeguards appropriate to the record type. General website signups must not be silently attributed to a location-specific campaign. Narrow-phone acquisition flows must be checked for wrapping, overlap, clipped actions, and unusable search or checkout controls.
